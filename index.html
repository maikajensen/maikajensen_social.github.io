Airbnb in Copenhagen
Analyzing Listings & Predicting Prices

Have you ever tried Airbnb? If not, check it out! Finding the right place to stay during your travel can be a time consuming process, having to research a lot of different hotels on many different websites, it can be a maze. What do you actually want with your travel? Is it to have a wonderful stay at a tourist hotel full of swan-shaped towels and a litte chocolate on the pillow, or is it to have a unique authentic stay, where you get the full experience of your travel destination? That answer is quite easy, right?

With Airbnb, you can find unique places rented out by locals from your destination, or you can rent out your own place and help visiters get the most authentic stay in your city, and at the same time earn a some money. Isn't that amazing? If Airbnb sounds appealing to you and you consider the idea of renting out your place – and you live in Copenhagen – you have come to the right place!

New to the Airbnb Market?

Is the thought of renting your place out totally new for you, and do you not know where and how to get started? Let us help you! We provide you with all the 'need to know' and 'nice to know' before getting comfortable renting out your housing. We show you the distribution of listings all around Copenhagen, show you how huge Airbnb has become the last couple of years, give you insights into when it is most optimal to rent out your housing, guide you through the different neighbourhoods of Copenhagen to see which areas are trending, and provide you with information about your competitors' prices. After collecting all this information, you are almost ready to attack the Airbnb market and become a host. But what price can you take for a night at your place? Don't worry! We have collected a lot of information about the existing Airbnb listings in Copenhagen, and trained a machine learning model to offer you a guided price for your place. You just have to fill in the properties of your housing, and here you go! Now you are ready to attack the Airbnb market.

When to rent your place out?

The majority of people travelling to Denmark and Copenhagen, come durng the summertime. The Dansih summer with a large Danish softice with guf and a Carlsberg beer is not to resist, which is visible on the yearly distribution of visiters who rent a housing in Copenhagen with Airbnb. Absolutly most visiters are seen from May to September, and fewest in January and February. This is illustrated in the visualization below, where the total number of reviews in Copenhagen from January 2012 until December 2019 are seen. As you see, you came at the right time! Airbnb is trending right now!

A review is obtained after an end estate rental, and is the same as one or more nights, namely a stay. Eight yearly distributions are visuable, all showing the same tendency. Most people are renting their place in the summertime, and more and more visiters are seen though the years from 2012 until 2019! The same color combination is used though a year, where the darkest blue illustrates January and the yellow illustrates December.

Having a closer look at only a single year, namely 2019, a lot of spikes are seen in the visualization below. Again a small color palette is used, now to illustrate one week. Yellow is now mondays and the darker blue colors illustrate the weekends. Most reviews are defenetly seen sundays to tuesdays. As mentioned, the plot illustates the number of reviews and not the the exact start or end date of a stay. For this reason, the weekly pattern becomes a little skeewed with a few days, having the assumption that most rentings are seen in the weekends. This assumption then still remains true.

So if you decide to rent out your place and become an Airbnb host then be prepared every weekend, and especially thoughout the summer. You will probably get a guest or two!

Competitors Prices

Before attacking the Airbnb market as a new host, it is worth investigating the prices of listings around Copenhagen and within your own neighbourhood. All investigated prices are 'per night' and in DKK. First an overview of all listing prices!

Absolutly most listings lie in the price range from 300 DKK to 1000 DKK. Most prices are set to the nearest 100, 500 or 1000 indicated by the strong majority of observations at these prices. Only a smaller number of listings are priced higher than 2000 DKK per night. A map locates the different listings just seen in the jitterplot. From zooming in and moving around, it is apparent that the more central the location of the listing is, the more expensive the renting price is per night. Opposite, the farther we get from the center of Copenhagen, the cheaper a listing becomes per night. What is also noticable is, that listings near water of any kind, whether it is the sea, the lakes of Copenhagen or the canals, are more expensive. This illustrative map ranges shows prices from 0 DKK to 2000 DKK (blue to magenta colors). Listings more expensive than 2000 DKK are marked magenta as well, indicating that these housings are very expensive per night!.

We know that Indre by has the highest listing prices per night, and that the listing prices become lower the further we get from the center. A little zoom on the different neighbourhoods in the map independently makes you able to go around tracking the information you are looking for. Check out your own neighbourhood and compare the prices with other neighbourhoods'. This same investigation can be performed on the interactive visualization below. From here it is clear that the regions providing the lowest prices are Bispebjerg, Vanløse and Brønshøj-Husum being the more western neighbourhoods of Copenhagen.

Okay, so for now our assumptions are that you can charge a higher price for your housing if it is located close to the center of Copenhagen or near water of any kind. Let's see if a Machine Learning algorithm, namely K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), can prove our assumptions drawn from the previous visualizations. KNN is a classification algorithm, here used to find out at what locations, all around Copenhagen, you can charge more than 1000 DKK per night! Solely based on the location! Pretty nice, right?

Wow! A grid full of points and full of super cool information! In the red areas you can charge more than 1000 DKK per night for your place solely based on the location! Assumptions proved! You can take a higher price per night, if your housing is located in the centrum of Copenhagen or near water.

Price Guide - Predict the Price of your own Housing

So now you are probably interested in what price you can charge for a night at your place through Airbnb? We have combined the information from all the previous analyses with even more information from the data to predict the price of your housing. The price is guidence and you can choose to make use of it or not. The price will be given in DKK and is per night. For predciting the listing price we make use of the most recent Airbnb prices, so that the estimated price will match the present prices on the market.

Our model is based on the Random Forest Machine Learning Classifier. If you want to learn more about the method and the trained prediction model, then check out our explainer notebook. You find it from the link in the bottom of this website.

For predicting the price of your place, some features have more influence than others. Features as the location (latitude, longitude, neighbourhood), the property type, the number of bedrooms, the number of beds and the number of days you want your listing to be available in the next 30 days, 60 days, 90 days and 365 days on the Airbnb market have a larger impact on the price. The amenities that you offer in your housing are also important. Some of the most important ones are diswasher, TV and washer.

Try to fill out the specifications of your rental and predict the price you can charge per night! Good luck!
